# Quarantined AI concept candidates

These two AI-generated concept illustrations were rejected during the v6
scientific-defect audit and MUST NOT be referenced from public components.
They are preserved here so Nicol can see the rejected options.

## journey-provenance.png
- Rejection reason: contains an invented / distorted map of Latin America
  (imagined coastline) and pseudo-handwritten text that reads as illegible
  gibberish. The provenance module cannot show a false map + fake labels
  as a "documentary or conceptual" candidate — provenance credibility is
  the point of the module.
- Replacement: A7 documentary photograph (Wikimedia — proper insect
  pinning and label order, CC BY-SA 4.0) — see public/media/documentary/.

## reference-evidence-map.png
- Rejection reason: butterflies illustrated are orange-and-black
  nymphalid look, NOT Caligo owl butterflies; world map shows dots on
  every continent implying global Caligo distribution which is wrong
  (Caligo is Neotropical). Chromosome-graphic bands read as real
  comparative genomic data but are decorative. Fake specimen labels.
- Replacement: code-native SVG explainer (per GPT Pro brief §824-825,
  homepage_selected_visuals.evidence_branching = "code-native"). No AI or
  documentary photo for this module.

## journey-voucher.png (v6 audit)
- Rejection reason: the digital metadata sheet paired with the voucher
  shows a world map with highlighted regions on Eurasia/Africa, not
  Latin America. For a Caligo journey step about voucher-to-data linkage,
  a false-geography map is disqualifying.
- Replacement: A7 documentary (Wikimedia — proper insect pinning + label
  order, CC BY 4.0, Trapezites sciron labels) — see public/media/documentary/a7-specimen-labels.jpg.

## pilot-panacea.png (v6 audit)
- Rejection reason: the illustrated butterfly looks like a Heliconius-type
  longwing (elongated wings, red/yellow banding), NOT Panacea prola (which
  should read as a robust nymphalid "red flasher" with a broad hindwing
  and a red-and-black underside colour block). Taxon mismatch on the
  central subject of a species-specific pilot is disqualifying.
- Replacement: A5 documentary (Wikimedia — Panacea prola underside,
  CC BY-SA 2.0) alone. No AI concept candidate for the Panacea pilot.
